Why secondhand actually helps

4 min read · The Planet

The sustainability pitch for secondhand clothing gets thrown around a lot, often without much explanation. Here's what buying used actually does — and where the claims get oversold.

What buying secondhand genuinely does

  • Extends the life of something that already exists. Every garment already carries the water, energy, and labour cost of being made. Keeping it in use longer means that cost is spread across more wear, rather than a new item requiring all of that again from scratch.
  • Reduces what goes to landfill or incineration. Clothing that would otherwise be discarded gets a second life instead of adding to textile waste.
  • Slightly lowers demand for new production. Every secondhand purchase is one less new item bought, even if the effect on any single brand's output is small.

Where the claims get oversold

Buying one secondhand jacket won't meaningfully offset fast fashion on its own — the impact is real but incremental, not dramatic. And secondhand shopping isn't a free pass to buy more than you need; the environmental benefit comes from replacing new purchases, not stacking on top of them.

The honest version

Buying secondhand is one sensible habit among several, not a single fix. If you're buying less overall, keeping items in use longer, and choosing secondhand where it makes sense, the combined effect adds up — even if no single purchase is doing all the work.
