What Does "Very Good" Actually Mean on Vinted?

4 min read · Buying Guide

If you've bought more than a couple of things on Vinted, you've probably had this experience: a listing says "very good condition," and it turns up with a stain on the collar or a bobbled cuff. You're not imagining it — condition labels on Vinted cause more buyer frustration than almost anything else on the platform, and it's worth understanding exactly what each one is meant to mean, and where the gap between "meant to" and "actually does" tends to open up.

What the labels are officially meant to mean

Vinted defines four condition categories:

  • New with tags — never worn, tags still attached.
  • Very good — gently used, in excellent condition, with only minor imperfections that don't affect how it looks or works.
  • Good — regularly used, shows wear, but still works well for its purpose.
  • Satisfactory — well used, with clear signs of wear and imperfections, but still functional.

Where it goes wrong in practice

The categories are subjective by design, and sellers new to the platform often don't calibrate them the way experienced buyers expect. A stain that a seller considers "minor" might be a dealbreaker for a buyer expecting "very good" to mean close to unworn. This exact frustration shows up constantly in buyer communities — people specifically complaining about sellers marking stained or bobbled items as "very good," when in their view that condition should mean no visible marks at all.

How to protect yourself as a buyer

  • Read the photos, not just the label. A written condition tag is a summary — the photos are the actual evidence. Zoom into cuffs, collars, soles, and seams before trusting the label alone.
  • Ask before buying if anything's unclear. A seller unwilling to answer a direct question about wear or flaws is a red flag in itself.
  • Remember you're protected either way. If an item turns up in meaningfully worse condition than described, that's exactly the kind of case Vinted's Buyer Protection exists for.

How we grade our own listings

We treat "very good" as meaning exactly that — no stains, no bobbling, no notable wear, something you'd be happy to wear straightaway. If a piece has any flaws worth mentioning, we say so directly in the description and photograph the flaw itself, rather than leaving you to spot it after it arrives. If you ever have a question about a specific piece before buying, just ask.
